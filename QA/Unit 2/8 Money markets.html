Competency Test (Random Difficulty Questions)

Question 1

[1013382] Which of the following best describes a certificate of deposit?
A
It is a corporate-issued discount instrument that pays no coupon
B
It is a government issued discount instrument that pays no coupon
C
It is a bank issued tradable time deposit
D
It is a corporate-issued time draft
The correct answer is: C - It is a bank issued tradable time deposit

Explanation

A certificate of deposit (CD) is a bearer instrument representing a time deposit. They do pay interest all at once at the maturity date (bullet).

Question 2

[1013375] The government issues a 180 day Treasury bill for a price of £9,700 with a par value of £10,000. What annual return does this represent to an investor who holds it to maturity, calculated on a simple basis?
A
3.00%
B
3.16%
C
6.27%
D
6.00%
The correct answer is: C - 6.27%

Explanation

You buy a T-bill for 9,700 and redeem for 10,000 making 300 profit on a 9,700 investment. 300/9,700 = 0.03093 This profit is over 180 days, so turn into an annual rate: 0.03093 x 365/180 = 0.0627 or 6.27%

Question 3

A
To control the amount of cash in the banking system

To control foreign exchange rates

To control unemployment
D
To promote speculation

Question 4

[1013374] A purchases a CD from B in the secondary market. The credit risk to A is:
A
The risk of B defaulting
B
The risk of the issuer defaulting
C
Foreign exchange risk
D
The risk of interest rates changing
The correct answer is: B - The risk of the issuer defaulting

Explanation

A CD is supported by a deposit held by the issuer. The risk is that the issuer defaults.

Question 5

[1013367] The Edmonton Energy Company buys a CD via the secondary market from the Ilford Ink Company. The credit risk associated with this CD for the Edmonton Energy Company is:
A
The risk of the issuer defaulting
B
The risk of the Ilford Ink Company defaulting
C
The risk of an increase in interest rates
D
Re-investment risk at the maturity of the CD
The correct answer is: A - The risk of the issuer defaulting

Explanation

The issuers of CDs are banks. The credit risk lies with the issuing bank defaulting and not paying the holder of the CD the proceeds at maturity.

A
12 months to five years

Question 9

[1013369] One of the key roles played by Treasury bills is:
A
To be an instrument of controlling the amount of cash in the banking system
B
To raise long-term government finance
C
To influence transactions in the UK equity market
D
To help reduce the rate of inflation
The correct answer is: A - To be an instrument of controlling the amount of cash in the banking system

Explanation

The Bank of England uses T-bills to conduct open-market operations (OMOs). During OMOs, the bank is buying or selling T-bills in the London money market to either inject or remove cash to/from the banking system.
