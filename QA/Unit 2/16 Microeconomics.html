Question 14

[1013743] In equilibrium, for a firm in perfect competition:
A
Average revenue equals price, but is less than marginal revenue
B
Average revenue equals price, but is greater than marginal revenue
C
Average revenue is less than price, but is greater than marginal revenue
D
Average revenue equals price and marginal revenue
The correct answer is: D - Average revenue equals price and marginal revenue

Explanation

A firm in perfect competition is a 'price-taker'. This means its output does not alter price or demand. In other words, the demand curve for the good produced is flat - perfectly elastic. Any increase in output will be sold at the same price, making marginal revenue and average revenue the same as the price.

Question 15

[1013751] Two commodities have a positive cross-elasticity of demand. These commodities will be:
A
Substitutes
B
Complements
C
Joint demand
D
A product together with its by-product
The correct answer is: A - Substitutes

Explanation

Substitutes have a POSITIVE cross price elasticity, e.g. if the price of margarine rises the demand for butter may rise. Complementary goods have a NEGATIVE cross price elasticity of demand, e.g. if the price of printers falls, the demand for printer ink may rise. Joint demand (not on the syllabus) refers to two different but interdependent goods, e.g. livestock and leather. These, like complementary goods, would deplay negative cross elasticity, as would a product and its by-product.

Question 17

[1066891] In a competitive industry, the firm's short run supply curve is:
A
The short run marginal cost curve above the average total cost curve
B
Its short run marginal cost curve above its short run average variable cost curve
C
Its short run marginal cost curve above its long run average variable cost curve
D
The long run marginal cost curve above the average total cost curve
The correct answer is: B - Its short run marginal cost curve above its short run average variable cost curve

Explanation

A supply curve is a curve showing the quantity a firm wishes to supply at each price. The firm's short run supply curve is the output level associated in the short run with different prices. In a competitive industry, the firm's short run supply curve is its short run marginal cost curve above its short run average variable cost curve. In the long run, the supply curve is the long run marginal cost curve above the average total cost curve.

Question 36

[1013745] Supernormal profit may be defined as:
A

C
Profits over and above what would be considered normal for the industry
D
Profits in excess of all opportunity costs and explicit costs
The correct answer is: D - Profits in excess of all opportunity costs and explicit costs

Profits in excess of explicit costs is called accounting profit. Profits in excess of explicit costs and opportunity costs is supernormal or economic profit. Question 38

[1013765] Which of the following is not true about a profit-maximising monopolist?
A
The monopolist will produce where MC = MR
B
The monopolist will not necessarily produce at minimum average cost
C
The monopolist will face a downward sloping marginal revenue curve
D
The monopolist will always produce on the inelastic portion of the demand curve
The correct answer is: D - The monopolist will always produce on the inelastic portion of the demand curve

Explanation

A monopolist will actually always produce on the ELASTIC portion of the demand curve, as this is the portion where an increase in output will lead to a further increase in total revenue. Moving into the inelastic portion will reduce total revenues. For monolpolists, producing where MC = MR gives profit maximisation and, because they make supernormal profits, the average total costs will be covered whether they are producing where they are minimised or not. In order to increase quantity demanded, the monopolist will need to reduce prices; this creates a downward sloping marginal revenue curve.
