Question 20

[1013232] An investor buys 80 shares at £3.89 each. One year later a total dividend of £15 is received, followed by a dividend of £21 at the end of the second year. The shares are then sold for £3.75 each. What is the internal rate of return on this investment?
A
0.00%
B
2.00%
C
3.00%
D
4.00%
The correct answer is: D - 4.00%

Explanation

The IRR is the rate at which the present value of inflows is equal to the present value of outflows: First identify the inflows and outflows. The investor spends £3.89 x 80 shares = £311.20 (outflows). The investor receives £15 after 1 year (inflow). The investor receives £21 after 2 years (inflow) and also sells the shares at this point £3.75 x 80 = £300 (inflow). We need a discount rate for the inflows that will give us a result equal to the initial outflow. So: £311.20 = [£15 / (1 + r)] + [£321 / (1 + r)^2] At this stage, only substitution will work, and 4% gives us the correct answer.

Question 29

[1012698] What is the price of a £5,000 quarterly annuity payable over five years, if the interest rate is 8% per annum?
A
£49,090
B
£81,757
C
£20,439
D
£67,297
The correct answer is: B - £81,757

Explanation

The 5,000 cash flow occurs quarterly over the five years making 20 periods (5 x 4). The period rate is 2% (8% / 4). We use the annuity formula here to achieve the present value of this series of cash flows: PV = CF x 1 / r(1-1 / (1+r)^n) PV = 5000 x 1 / 0.02(1-1 / 1.02^20) = 81,757.
