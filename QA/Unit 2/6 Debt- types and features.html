Competency Test (Random Difficulty Questions)

Question 1

[1013304] Calculate the conversion premium of a bond priced at £108, with a nominal value of £100 that will convert into 40 shares priced at 250p per share. Express this as a percentage. You should answer this question in percent in the following format without any symbols: 0
The correct answer is: 8 - 8

Explanation

Effective cost per share via the convertible debt is: Price of bond / Potential number of shares £108 / 40 = £2.70 This is an additional 20p paid over the current share price. Expressed as a percentage of the current share price 20p / £2.50 = 0.08 or 8%.

Question 2

[1013313] What is coupon stripping?
A
Selling bonds in the open market
B
The process of separating and selling individual cash flows of bonds
C
The sale and repurchase of gilts
D
The process of applying for gilts in the DMO
The correct answer is: B - The process of separating and selling individual cash flows of bonds

Explanation

Coupon stripping is the process of separating and selling individual cash flows of bonds - thus the principal amount and each coupon can be traded separately.

Question 3

C
They trade on the London Stock Exchange
D

The correct answer is: B - They are issued in a currency other than that of the home state

A Eurobond is where the nationality of the issuer, the country of issue and the currency are all different. They are over-the-counter instruments and are regulated by the International Capital Markets Association.

Question 7

Do not attract SDRT
A
I and III
B
I, II and III
C
II, III and IV
D

Explanation

Question 17

Explanation

Effective cost of share via convertible debt is: Bond price / Potential number of shares £110 / 40 shares = £2.75 per share The additional amount paid via the bond route is £2.75 - £2.56 = 19p. Expressed as a percentage of the current share price, is 19p/£2.56 = 0.0742 or 7.42%

Question 21

[1013318] What effect does a call provision have on a corporate bond?
A
It allows the issuer of the bond to redeem the bond at its discretion
B
It allows the issuer of the bond to alter the par value at its discretion
C
It allows the issuer of the bond to alter the coupon rate at its discretion
D
It allows the holder of the bond to redeem the bond at the holder's discretion
The correct answer is: A - It allows the issuer of the bond to redeem the bond at its discretion

Explanation

A call provision gives the company the right to BUY bonds back: i.e. the right to redeem. A 'put provision' allows the holder to force the issuer of bonds to redeem them early: i.e. the investor can SELL them back early.
