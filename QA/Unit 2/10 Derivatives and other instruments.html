Question 1

[1013408] Consider two put options with different strike prices. The option with the lower strike price will normally:
A
Have the higher premium
B
Have the lower premium
C
Have greater volatility
D
Both options will have the same premium
The correct answer is: B - Have the lower premium

Explanation

A put option is the right to sell. At any given time the option with the higher strike price will be more valuable. Volatility is a characteristic of the underlying asset.

A

B
£10,000 loss
C
£2,000 profit
D
£2,000 loss

Question 25

[1013434] An investor buys a put with an exercise price of 90p for a premium of 21p. What is the maximum possible overall profit?
A
21
B
69
C
90
D
111
The correct answer is: B - 69

Explanation

The theoretical maximum profit is obtained by selling the underlying asset, when worthless, for the exercise price. In this question, this would lead to a gain of 90p. However, in order to receive the option to sell, a premium of 21p has to be paid. The overall profit is therefore 90p - 21p = 69p.
