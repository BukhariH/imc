Question 2

[1013276] Which of the following best describes an intermediaries offer as a means of obtaining a listing?
A
Placing of an issue that is below the threshold for a placing
B
A placing achieved by using more than one broker
C
An offer for sale to the general public
D
An offer for subscription
The correct answer is: B - A placing achieved by using more than one broker

Explanation

In an intermediaries offer, the original broker sells the new shares to a selection of other brokers, who in turn place them with their favoured institutional investors.

Question 3

Question 4

A
Company bond holders
B
Existing shareholders
C
Company employees
D
A company's auditors
The correct answer is: B - Existing shareholders

Explanation

Pre-emptive rights (Companies Act 2006) ensure that new shares are offered to existing shareholders first, in proportion to their existing holdings.

Question 5

[1013272] Which of the following is true of rights issues?
A

B
The shares are never underwritten
C
The shares must be taken up by the existing shareholders
D
It allows existing shareholders to retain their proportional holding in the company
The correct answer is: D - It allows existing shareholders to retain their proportional holding in the company

Explanation

Rights issues are made at a discount to the prevailing market price, in proportion to existing shareholdings. Existing shareholders have a choice: take up the rights or sell them.

Question 6

[1013286] A company makes a 1 for 3 rights issue at 25p. If the cum-rights price is 55p, what are the ex-rights price and the value of a right, respectively?
A
50p and 25p
B
47.5p and 22.5p
C
32.5p and 7.5p
D
30p and 5p
The correct answer is: B - 47.5p and 22.5p

Explanation

Before rights issue: 3 shares @ 55p = £1.65 Rights issue: 1 share @ 25p = £0.25 After rights issue: 4 shares worth a total of £1.90 Therefore, ex rights price = £1.90 / 4 shares = 47.5p Value of right = 47.5p - 25p = 22.5p.

Question 12

Question 14

[1013269] A scrip issue means:
A
An issue of ordinary shares with a redemption date
B
An issue of ordinary shares with a fixed dividend
C
An issue that will replace the current ordinary shares when they are redeemed
D
An issue of shares in proportion to current holdings
The correct answer is: D - An issue of shares in proportion to current holdings

Explanation

A scrip issue is another way of referring to a 'bonus' or 'capitalisation' issue. It is a share issue where the issuing company receives no proceeds. Shares must be offered to existing shareholders in proportion to their existing holdings.

Question 18

[1013271] What is true of an introduction as a means of obtaining a listing on the LSE?
I
New shares are issued
II
New money is raised
III
A relatively inexpensive way of obtaining a listing
A
I only
B
I and II
C
II only
D
III only
The correct answer is: D - III only

Explanation

With an introduction, a company gains all the benefits of a listing (e.g. higher profile, ability to raise capital in future, ability for shareholders to release value, etc.) without raising any capital. No new shares are issued, and it is relatively cheap due to fewer formalities (compared with an offer or placing).

Question 22

[1013295] Which of the following best describes the theoretical value of a nil paid right?
A
The subscription price less the cum-rights price
B
The cum rights price less the ex-rights price
C
The ex-rights price less the subscription price
D
The cum-rights price less the subscription price
The correct answer is: C - The ex-rights price less the subscription price

Explanation

During a rights issue, shareholders can trade the right to subscribe for new shares. The value of this right is referred to as the nil paid price. The theoretical nil paid price is given by the answer in 'the ex-rights price less the subscription price'.

A
Nil
B
Increase by £150

Decrease by £150
D
