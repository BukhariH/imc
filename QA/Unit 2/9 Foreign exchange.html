Question 1

[1001329] If the sterling vs. the dollar spot rate is 1.7180-1.7270 and the three-month forward rate adjustment is 42-44, how many dollars need to be sold to raise £150,000 in three months' time? You should answer this question by typing the correct numbers in the following format without symbols: 000,000
The correct answer is: 259,710 - 259,710

Explanation

The investor wishes to sell dollars (so buys pounds). As the investor is buying base, the higher rate is used: 1.7270 US dollar. The forward rate adjustment is 42-44 (low number-high number) telling us that adding the adjustment will widen the spread. Add forward rate adjustment to the spot rate: 1.7270 + 0.0044 = 1.7314 Finally calculate the number of dollars that need to be sold to buy £150,000 forward: £150,000 x 1.7314 = $259,710

Question 2

[1001325] Which of the following is false of the currency markets?
A
Both spot transactions and forward transactions are offered
B
All transactions are guaranteed by the International Currency Clearing House
C
There is no fixed market place
D
Brokers operate to link buyers with sellers
The correct answer is: B - All transactions are guaranteed by the International Currency Clearing House

Explanation

The currency market is a huge over-the-counter market - there is no fixed market place. Settlement-of-currency transactions are processed directly between the counterparties to the trade. There is no central counterparty operating in the markets protecting against default. The brokers operating in the currency markets are called 'money brokers'. Spot settlement for currency transactions is T+2.

Question 6

[1001326] If the Japanese yen is quoted at a premium to sterling on the forward foreign exchange markets, then the forward quote should be:
A
Added to the spot rate
B
Deducted from the spot rate
C
The same as the spot rate
D
Impossible to say from the given information
The correct answer is: B - Deducted from the spot rate

Explanation

A premium on yen in the forward markets implies that the yen is getting more expensive. You will therefore be getting LESS yen for your £1 sterling. Although it might at first seem counter-intuitive, a premium is DEDUCTED from spot while a discount is ADDED.

Question 8

[1001828] The current spot quote is $1.7920-$1.7930 per £1. The forward quote is 30-28 pips. At what price can a client buy a US dollar forward? You should answer this question by typing in the following format without symbols: 0.0000
The correct answer is: 1.7890 - 1.7890

Explanation

The spot rate needs to be adjusted by the forward rate adjustment. As the adjustment is 30-28 (high number - low number), in order to widen the spread in the forward market we subtract the adjustment from the spot rate. (1.7920 - 1.7930) - (0.0030 - 0.0028) = 1.7890 - 1.7902 The rate to buy USD (sell GBP) will be 1.7890.

Question 9

[1012670] You invested $1,200 when the exchange rate was $1.50 = £1. Two years later, your investment is worth $1,485 and the exchange rate is $1.65 = £1. What is the time-weighted average rate of return in terms of GBP?
A
6.07%
B
11.88%
C
6.25%
D
11.24%
The correct answer is: A - 6.07%

Explanation

Investment in sterling = $1,200 / 1.50 = £800 This is now worth $1,485 / 1.65 = £900 Your total return is 12.50% (100 / 800). To find the geometric average, we take the square root of 1.125 (1 plus 12.5%), which gives us 6.07%.

Explanation

The spot rate is $1.4735-1.4770 and the forward rate adjustment is 15-10.
