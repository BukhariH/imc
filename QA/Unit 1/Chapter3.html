Competency Test (Random Difficulty Questions)

Question 1

[1014069] Which of the following statements is true with respect to cancellation rights on the sale to a retail client of a packaged product?
A
None required
B
Cancellation rights should be offered
C
Cancellation and withdrawal rights of 21 days are compulsory
D
Cancellation rights may be offered for a minimum period specified by the firm
The correct answer is: B - Cancellation rights should be offered

Explanation

This is the best answer. Depending upon the type of product, cancellation periods can vary between 14 and 30 calendar days.

Question 2

[1013975] Durable media are those which according to COBS:
A
Can be accessed by a person and will last at least three years
B
Can be accessed by a person and which allows unchanged reproduction of the information stored on it
C
Must be provided for the use of the client
D
Are a generally and publicly accessible website
The correct answer is: B - Can be accessed by a person and which allows unchanged reproduction of the information stored on it

Explanation

There is no specification of the durability of the medium in terms of years. The reference is to the fact that it must be durable for sufficient time that is adequate for the information contained within the medium. It may be offered to the client, though there is no necessity of provision. The website here suggests that it is open to all and sundry and therefore would not be a reasonable method of communicating with clients.

Question 3

[1013955] A retail client would normally expect to get a periodic statement of their non-leveraged security based account at least:
A
Annually
B
Every six months
C
Every three months
D
Whenever a transaction is executed
The correct answer is: B - Every six months

Explanation

A retail client will generally get a periodic statement every six months, though it is possible that it can be requested as a quarterly statement. If the portfolio is leveraged then the periodic statement is at least monthly. In the case of the simplest portfolios, the client can get the statement annually, but this is only if information about transactions is given on a transaction by transaction basis.

Question 8

[1014061] All of the following are true regarding the rules on client money, except:
A
Interest earned on client money need not be paid to clients
B
Professional clients may opt out of client money protection providing they have given written consent and the firm has warned them of the risks of opting out of the rules
C
All client accounts must be reconciled on a daily basis
D
Money due to the firm to settle an account where the firm has dealt as principal with the client is not client money
The correct answer is: C - All client accounts must be reconciled on a daily basis

Explanation

The reconciliation is as often as necessary, which is not necessarily every day. NOTE: the interest earned on client money need not be paid to clients. The firm can keep any interest on the money - although they would need to notify a retail client that this is the case. Only if the firm chooses to pay the interest to client, will the interest will become client money.

Question 34

[1016029] Which of the following is client money?
A
A transaction fee due to the firm
B
Money deposited in an approved bank account
C
Settlement charge due to the firm
D
Money held in excess to a client's obligations
The correct answer is: D - Money held in excess to a client's obligations

Explanation

Any money 'due to the firm' is not client money. Approved banks do not need to treat money deposited with them as client money. Where a firm sells assets held as security against client obligations, the amount equal to the obligation is the firm's money. However, anything in excess of this obligation has to be returned to the client or treated as client money.

The employment history is not required; rather, the client's financial situation, including real estate, sources of income, liquid assets etc. Experience in derivatives is only necessary as part of a decision to recommend that type of instrument.
