Question 2

[1054241] Mrs A is a higher rate taxpayer, and has received £300 interest on her deposit account. At the start of the year the account held £25,000. If Mrs A's husband has not used his NISA allowance, how much tax would they save if they had put as much as possible of the bank deposit in an NISA in Mr A's name instead? Assume the interest rate in the NISA is the same as in Mrs A's deposit account.
A
£375
B
£150
C
£69.12
D
£90
The correct answer is: D - £90

Explanation

Mrs A received £300. This will be net of 20%. Gross interest = 300 x 100 / 80 = £375 We take the gross interest £375 and work out the proportion available from a full NISA subscription: £375 x 15,000 / 25,000 = £225 Tax no longer due on this interest: £225 x 40% = £90

Question 18

Explanation

Dividends are received net of a 10% tax credit. This is the tax that an investor, irrespective of the tax bracket they are in, is deemed to have paid. The tax credit on a 16p dividend can be calculated as follows: 16p x 10/90 = 1.778p The total tax credit for the 500 share holding would be: 1.778 x 500 = 889p or £8.89 As the examiner specified that the answer was to be expressed in pounds the answer is £8.89

Question 22

[1051091] Craig, having used up his nil-rate band for inheritance tax, makes the following gifts: Gift 1: £35,000 to a discretionary trust. Gift 2: £50,000 to a registered charity. Gift 3: £325,000 to his son. Which of the following is true?
A
Gift 1 would trigger an immediate tax charge
B
Gifts 1 and 3 are gifts with reservation
C
Gifts 2 and 3 are potentially exempt transfers
D
Gifts 1 and 2 are chargeable lifetime transfers
The correct answer is: A - Gift 1 would trigger an immediate tax charge

Explanation

A gift to a discretionary trust is a chargeable lifetime transfer, triggering an immediate tax charge. It is liable for 20% IHT and then further tax if the donor dies within seven years. A gift to a charity is exempt. The gift to the son would be a potentially exempt transfer, liable for tax if the donor dies within seven years of the gift. A gift with reservation is when the donor gifts the asset but continues to benefit from it. There is no indication of any of these gifts falling into that category.
