Question 8

[1015171] When a UK listed company releases profit figures and/or dividends which of the following methods do the FCA's listing rules require the company to use?
A
By holding a press conference on the day of the announcement before the market opens
B
By informing a primary information provider (PIP) without delay
C
By sending a circular to all shareholders on the register
D
By announcement at the next general meeting
The correct answer is: B - By informing a primary information provider (PIP) without delay

Explanation

All announcements made by listed companies must be made to a Primary Information Provider (PIP) - also known as Regulatory Information Services (RISs). PIPs/RISs are approved by the FCA and include the London Stock Exchange's Regulatory News Service (RNS), Business Wire and Newswire.

Question 9

[1065534] Under which of the following pieces of legislation would companies that trade on the AIM market be required to provide half yearly reports?
A
AIM rules for companies
B
Companies Act
C
Listing Rules
D
IFRS
The correct answer is: A - AIM rules for companies

Explanation

An AIM company must prepare a half-yearly report in respect of the six month period from the end of the financial period for which financial information has been disclosed in its admission document and at least every subsequent six months thereafter (apart from the final period of six months preceding its accounting reference date for its annual audited accounts). All such reports must be notified without delay and in any event not later than three months after the end of the relevant period.

Question 13

[1065557] Which of the following is not true in relation to the Corporate Governance Code?

B

C

D

The correct answer is: C - It encourages strong leadership by allowing directors to set their own salary to a level that motivates them to run the company successfully and ensures retention

Question 26

[1065533] Which of the following is required for an official LSE listing?
A
A market cap of £700,000
B
A 33% free float
C
3 years working capital
D
5 years accounts
The correct answer is: A - A market cap of £700,000

Explanation

The issuer must have published consolidated, independently audited accounts covering at least three years. The expected market value of all securities issued by the company, and to be listed, must be at least £700,000 for shares and £200,000 for debt securities. (There are exceptions where the amount of debt securities is not fixed, and also if the securities of the same class are already listed). At least 25% of the shares must be available for public trading, i.e., not be held by directors or significant shareholders (5%+). This is known as free float.

Question 27

[1015150] At what level is a mandatory bid required according to the Takeover Code?
A
3% of the voting capital of a company
B
5% of the voting capital of a company
C
15% of the voting capital of a company
D
30% of the voting capital of a company
The correct answer is: D - 30% of the voting capital of a company

Explanation

If anyone acquires 30% of the voting capital of a company they are obliged to make a compulsory, or mandatory bid for the remaining shares in the company. This bid must be at a price no lower than the highest price paid over the preceding 12 months.
