Question 1

[1012258] When a UK company pays a dividend to a foreign investor, what is the withholding tax payable?
A
10% of the gross dividend paid
B
20% of the gross dividend paid
C
32.5% of the dividend paid
D
40% of the gross dividend paid
The correct answer is: A - 10% of the gross dividend paid

Explanation

Dividends paid abroad by UK companies have only the normal 10% deducted at source. Foreign investors receiving UK dividends may claim the 10% from their own tax authority. UK resident investors may not claim back this 10%.

Question 5

A
Book entry transfer
B
Free delivery
C

Question 84

[1066761] Which of these would provide the least protection for income against inflation?
A
Conventional gilts
B
Equities
C
Bank Account
D
Overseas Equities
The correct answer is: A - Conventional gilts

Explanation

Over the long term average equity return has outperformed inflation, bank account interest is calculated on the BOE rate which can be changed in response to inflation and the rate benefits from compounding. Conventional gilts have a nominal capital and coupon value providing no protection against inflation. Index-linked gilts are far better than conventional gilts at coping with inflation.
