Question 1

[1046724] Where do corporate bonds mostly trade?
A
Exchanges
B
Liquidity pools

Question 2

[1016986] In the event of a default by an exchange member, which of the following is the best description of what will happen if the clearing house offers an independent guarantee?
A

B
The resources of the clearing house and the clearing house members will be used to close-out the position
C
The resources of the members of the clearing house only will be used
D

If an exchange is using an independent guarantee then after using clearing member margin, its resources will be used in the event of a default by a clearing member. ICE Clear Europe uses a mutual guarantee where both the clearing house and members' resources are used.

B

Question 36

[1066763] What is the role of a systematic internaliser?
A
To provide continuous liquidity in the markets by providing buy prices (BID) and sell prices (OFFER)
B
To provide intermediating services between other firms
C
Borrowing from institutional investors with large portfolios that are passively held
D
To facilitate a cross trade between clients
The correct answer is: D - To facilitate a cross trade between clients

Explanation

A broker may act on behalf of TWO clients. The broker may, therefore, buy from one client AND sell to another. This is known as a 'CROSS-TRADE'. Although in performing this trade the firm has effectively created a flat position for itself, it must report both sides of the trade into the exchange. In this situation the broker would be known as a systematic inernaliser.
