Question 1

[1012256] All of the following are covered by the insider dealing legislation except:
A
Futures
B
OTC traded interest rate and currency derivatives
C
Depository receipts
D
Warrants
The correct answer is: B - OTC traded interest rate and currency derivatives

Explanation

The insider dealing legislation relates specifically to abuses of information in respect of securities markets. This therefore includes instruments based on securities, such as stock index futures, share warrants and equity options. It does not relate to OTC markets, such as foreign exchange forward contracts. Additionally, the legislation does not cover assets with no secondary market, such as unit trusts.

Question 42

[1016032] Tom discloses inside information to Harry, and reminds Harry not to deal based on that information, but Harry does anyway. Who should be charged with insider dealing?
A
Tom, not Harry
B
Harry, not Tom
C
Neither Tom nor Harry
D
Both Tom and Harry
The correct answer is: B - Harry, not Tom

Explanation

Harry would be guilty of insider dealing. He was fully aware of the nature of the information and dealt anyway. Tom, although disclosing the information, reminded Harry not to deal. He could therefore use either of the following defences: He could have passed on the information in the normal course of business, or he could use the defence that he did not expect the recipient to deal. Both of these defences would be consistent with reminding Harry not to deal.

Question 84

[1066764] How are the values of exchange traded futures calculated for accounting purposes?
A
According to the market value of the underlying asset
B
According to the price that was agreed on the creation of the contract
C
According to the market value of the future
D
According to the value of previous contracts held to delivery
The correct answer is: C - According to the market value of the future

Explanation

Derivatives should be valued on fair value through profit or loss (FVTPL). In this way they are marked to market at the time of producing the financial statements and any profit or loss, realised or unrealised, is reflected in the profit and loss account. Derivative positions used as a hedge are not be covered under IAS39, because, in general, a derivative used as a hedge will be held to delivery and therefore its realisable value. For this reason, the price that was agreed on the creation of the contract will be value used on the financial statements.
