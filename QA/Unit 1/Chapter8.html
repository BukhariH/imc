A
Capital Gains Tax
B
Income Tax

Question 3

[1015244] In a pension scheme, which of the following is responsible for the Statement of Investment Principles?
A
The sponsoring company
B
The members
C
The trustees
D
The fund manager
The correct answer is: C - The trustees

Explanation

The SIP must set out in writing: the scheme specific funding requirements, the nature of investments in the fund and the risks of the fund. The SIP must be reviewed every three years.

Question 15

[1015240] Which of the following is/are likely to cause a pension fund manager to increase the proportion of equities in their portfolio?
I
An increase in the trustees' tolerance of risk
II
An increase in the average age of contributors to the fund
III
An increase in expected inflation
A
I and III
B
II and III
C
III only
D
All of them
The correct answer is: A - I and III

Explanation

Option II is incorrect because an increase in the average age of contributors would cause a shift into bonds rather than equities. Option I is correct; an increased tolerance for risk permits a greater allocation to equity and its higher long-run expected return. Option III is correct as inflation erodes the nominal value of bonds, whereas shares are more likely to see their prices rise with inflation.

Question 18

[1015286] Which of the following types of pensions best describes a situation where an agreed level of contributions are paid by an individual and are invested over time to give a return?

Defined benefit
B
Defined contribution
C
SERPS
