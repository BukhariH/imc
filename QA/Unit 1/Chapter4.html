Competency Test (Random Difficulty Questions)

Question 1

[1015060] If a complaint has been received by a firm and remains unresolved, how soon after the firm receives it can the client refer the complaint to the Financial Ombudsman?
A
Four weeks
B
Six weeks
C
Eight weeks
D
Six months
The correct answer is: C - Eight weeks

Explanation

The client has the right to refer the complaint to the Financial Ombudsman if it has not been resolved eight weeks after it has been received by the firm. The firm must also give the client a leaflet about the Financial Ombudsman service.

Question 2

[1049916] Which of the following comes under 'compulsory jurisdiction' of the Financial Ombudsman Service?
A
Plastic cards including credit card transaction
B
Mortgages
C
General insurance
D
Pension provision
The correct answer is: D - Pension provision

Explanation

Currently, the following can be considered under voluntary jurisdiction: lending on mortgages by non-FCA-authorised firms; other specified lending activity; providing payments on plastic cards (excluding store cards); accepting deposits and providing general insurance where the service was provided from elsewhere within the EEA but was directed at the UK.

Question 3

[1000396] Which of the following is UNTRUE in respect of the procedures of an authorised firm for dealing with client complaints?
A
Firms must have adequate internal controls put in place
B
Firms must refer all complaints to the regulator
C
The firm must remedy legitimate grievances
D
The firm has a duty to inform clients of other avenues of complaint
The correct answer is: B - Firms must refer all complaints to the regulator

Explanation

Internal complaints procedures must be in writing and cover the receipt of complaints, methods of response and how they will be investigated. Complaints should be handled by a member of staff with sufficient experience and authority, and who was not involved in the matter. If a complaint cannot be resolved, the firm must inform the client about their rights to complain to the Financial Ombudsman and provide the client with the explanatory leaflet about the Financial Ombudsman Service.

Question 13

[1000395] In what circumstances could a claim on the Financial Services Compensation Scheme be made?
A
When an authorised firm becomes insolvent
B
Exclusively in response to claims by retail clients
C
Breach of principles of business
D
As an alternative to mediation by the Financial Ombudsman Scheme
The correct answer is: A - When an authorised firm becomes insolvent

Explanation

The Financial Services Compensation Scheme was established to provide compensation where authorised persons and appointed representatives are unable to satisfy claims against them. Although the scheme mainly applies to retail clients, it can also apply to professional clients and eligible counterparties.

Question 15

A
Three weeks

Three months

Question 17

[1000401] A complaint is NOT considered resolved where:
I
The firm has made a final response
II
The complainant has indicated in writing acceptance of the firm's response
III
The complainant has not responded to the firm after eight weeks of the firm's written response
IV
The complainant has not responded to the firm within four weeks of the firm's written acknowledgement
A
I and II
B
II and III
C
I and IV
D
III and IV
The correct answer is: C - I and IV

Explanation

Sending out a final response, in itself, does not resolve a complaint. The firm has to wait for the client to accept the response before the complaint is resolved. However, often clients do not respond. If the client has not responded to a response after eight weeks, the firm can consider the complaint resolved. Once a complaint is resolved, no further action by the firm is necessary.

A
Once a year

Question 22

[1015067] The maximum financial award by the Financial Ombudsman is:
A
Established in each case
B
Up to £150,000
C
Up to €150,000 or equivalent
D
On a sliding scale
The correct answer is: B - Up to £150,000

Explanation

The maximum financial award a firm can make is £150,000. Awards over £150,000 can be requested from a firm, but the firm is not compelled to pay them.

Question 23

[1015071] Which of the following statements are true regarding complaints against a firm which is being investigated by the Financial Ombudsman?
I
During the investigation the firm must co-operate fully with the Ombudsman
II
There is no ceiling regarding the award the Ombudsman may make against the firm
III
Any money award made by the Ombudsman against the firm is binding on the firm
A
I and III
B
I and II
C
II and III
D
I, II and III
The correct answer is: A - I and III

Explanation

The rules for the Ombudsman state that the maximum award the Ombudsman may make is £150,000. If the Ombudsman feels that an amount in excess of the maximum is appropriate it can invite the firm to pay the balance. Awards made by the Ombudsman against the firm may be recovered or enforced through the courts.
