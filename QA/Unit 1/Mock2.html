Question 1

[1000370] The FCA enforcement officers are investigating a breach of regulation by an authorised firm. They have the power to all of the following except:
A
Enter the premises once they have given notice that is deemed to be appropriate
B
Demand printouts of computer data
C
Remove original documentation for further inspection
D
Interview employees whether they are approved or otherwise
The correct answer is: C - Remove original documentation for further inspection

Explanation

FCA officers can demand the production of documents, tapes, files, computer data or any other information required by the FCA. Although, reasonable notice is normally given by the FCA, officers can have access without notice during business hours. They can also demand that any employees make themselves available for interview.

Question 49

[1024423] Which of the following is LEAST likely to be considered a UK resident?
A
Someone who was non-UK resident in all of the previous three tax years and visits the UK for 45 days during this tax year
B
Someone who was UK resident in the previous tax year and visits the UK for only 16 days during this tax year
C
Someone who is resident for no more than 184 days during this tax year
D
Someone who has their only home in the UK but only uses it for 100 days each tax year
The correct answer is: A - Someone who was non-UK resident in all of the previous three tax years and visits the UK for 45 days during this tax year

Explanation

A person is non-resident if any of the following conditions are met: They visit the UK for fewer than 16 days during the tax year; or They are non-UK resident in all of the previous three tax years and visit the UK for fewer than 46 days during the tax year; or They work full-time overseas and visit the UK for fewer than 91 days during the tax year. A person is resident if Part A does not apply and any of the following conditions are met: They are present in the UK for 183 days or more during the tax year; or They have their only home in the UK, This must be for a period of at least 91 days and they must live there for at least 30 days; or They carry out full-time work in the UK.
